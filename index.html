Ada Lovelace

First computer programmer

"Ada Lovelace (Augusta Ada King, Countess of Lovelace, née Byron; 10 December 1815 – 27 November 1852) has often been called ‘the first computer programmer’ for her work on Charles Babbage’s early mechanical calculating machines, and is credited with inventing the first algorithm, and essentially conceptualising the modern computer – or ‘thinking machine’." (Loritz, 2020)
